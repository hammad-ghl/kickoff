AI for Dev Nitro – Contest Guideline 🚀

What is Dev Nitro? ⚡
Productivity, Quality and System Performance. Not features — solutions to real engineering pain. The best ones get rewarded and shipped to production.
What is This Contest About? 🏆
This is a focused internal engineering contest where individuals or teams identify a real problem and blocker in our SDLC and build a working AI-powered solution for it.

Contest Date: Monday, 9th March 2026
Three Focus Themes:
Velocity & Productivity: Automate repetitive tasks, accelerate requirements generation, design mockups, code generation, PR reviews, documentation.
Quality Enhancement: Enforce standards, catch issues early, contextual feedback during development.
Tools for System Performance (Early Detection): Detect regressions, bottlenecks, and failures before they hit production.

Prizes: 🥇 ₹50k | 🥈 ₹30k | 🥉 ₹20k + Bonus Prize

Submissions must demonstrate technical depth, practical feasibility, and measurable impact.
How to Pick a Problem & Build Your Project 🛠️
Picking a Problem:
Bring any idea that fits one of the 3 themes—there is no predefined library.
Focus on outcome: what will be better, faster, or eliminated as a result of your solution?
Design Doc First (Mandatory):
Submit a Design Doc before writing any code. It must cover:

Problem Statement: What breaks today, how often, and what's the business impact?
Proposed Solution: What you're building and why.
Tech Stack & Architecture: Tools, integrations, how it fits our stack.
Success Metrics: Quantifiable outcomes (e.g., 40% faster, zero manual steps).
Test Strategy: TDD plan, unit vs. integration coverage.
Production Plan: Deployment steps, rollback strategy, estimated timeline.
Risks & Mitigations: What could go wrong and how you've planned for it.

Engineering Standards & Acceptance Criteria ✅
Mandatory Rules:

Working functional prototype
Test coverage (Unit + Integration) mandatory.
Design Doc submitted 
Solution must fit one of the 3 defined themes.
No production impact during the demo — use staging or sandbox.


Code Quality Expectations:

Follows existing repo code style and linting rules.
No critical code analysis violations.
Error handling and logging in place.
README or inline docs so anyone can onboard and run it.

What Production-Ready Means Here:

Can be deployed without heroics.
Rollback strategy documented.
Doesn’t introduce new toil or tech debt.
Another engineer (not on your team) can understand and maintain it.
How to Present & Demo 🎤
Each submission gets a maximum of 25 20 minutes on contest day (Monday, 9th March). Be sharp and focused — no overruns.

Problem Statement (1-2 min): 
What is the pain?
How frequent? 
What’s the business impact if left unsolved?
Proposed Solution (1-2 min): 
Your approach, key design decisions, and why this solution over alternatives.
Functional Prototype Demo (3-5 min):
Live demo of the working solution. No slides substituting a demo.
Technical Deep Dive (2-3 min): 
Architecture, AI/tooling choices
how it integrates with our stack.
Production Plan & Timeline (1–2 min): 
What it takes to go live
Dependencies, team needed, estimated timeline.
Risk Assessment & ROI (1–2 min): 
What could go wrong, mitigations, and quantified impact — time saved, errors prevented, new capabilities unlocked.
Q&A (5 mins): 
Q&A from the Judges panel and audience.

Total: 25 20 minutes max.

Demo Rules
Must be a live, working demo — not a recorded video or static screenshots.
Use staging/sandbox data — no production data in demos.
Have a backup plan if a live demo fails (short recording acceptable as backup only).
Strict 25 20 - minute time limit — practice your run-through beforehand.

Rules & Compliance 📋
No real data in demos — use synthetic or anonymized data only. No customer PII, no production databases.
Disclose AI tools used — clearly state which models/APIs power your solution. Do not feed confidential data into external AI APIs.
No secrets in code — no hardcoded API keys, tokens, or credentials in any submitted code or demo.
Original work only — all code must be original or properly licensed. Submissions become internal company IP.
Fabricated metrics = disqualification — all claims must be reproducible and demonstrable during Q&A.

FAQs ❓
Can I work alone? 
Yes, solo entries are allowed.
Does my solution have to be AI-powered? 
It must relate to one of the 3 AI themes — pure AI or AI-assisted tooling both qualify.
What if my demo breaks on the day? 
A short backup recording is acceptable, but a working live demo should be your primary target.
What is the team size limit? 
Up to 5 members per team.
Does the team need the same org/team/co-hort (Dev, Product etc.)?
No, you can form your team as per your needs and have members from multiple departments.
Is this the same as a hackathon? 
No. This is a contest where participants get time till Deadline day to complete and present the solution. Design and quality are expected from day one.
Is this just limited to the Dev team?
No, this is open for all teams involved in Development at Highlevel; 
Product, Design, Dev and QA.
